Maya Plisetskaya

Maya Mikhailovna Plisetskaya was a Soviet ballet dancer, choreographer, ballet director, and actress.

Here's a time line of Maya Plisetskaya life:

  • 1925 - Born in Moscow, Russian
  • 1938- She went to school in Spitzbergen, where her father worked as an engineer. In 1938, he was executed during the Stalinist purges, while her mother, a silent-film actress, was deported to Kazakhstan
  • 1943 - She graduated from the choreographic school and joined the Bolshoi Ballet, where she would perform until 1990
  • 1947 - Maya's most acclaimed roles included Odette-Odilia in Swan Lake
  • 1953 - Her performances were recorded in the film Stars of the Russian Ballet
  • 1958 - She was honoured with the title of the People's Artist of the USSR and married the young composer Rodion Shchedrin, in whose subsequent fame she shared
  • 1960 - Maya Plisetskaya was proclaimed the prima ballerina assoluta of the Bolshoi Theatre
  • 1961 - Plisetskaya performed in a number of countries and was a guest artist with the Paris Opéra. Maya's most acclaimed roles Aurora in Sleeping Beauty
  • 1972 - Her first choreography was Anna Karenina
  • 1975 - she also performed with the Ballet du XXe Siècle of Brussels
  • 1980 - Plisetskaya and Shchedrin spent much time abroad, where she worked as the artistic director of the Rome Opera Ballet and the Spanish National Ballet of Madrid
  • 1993-She and her husband, composer Rodion Shchedrin, eventually settled in Germany
  • 1994 - She has been presiding over the annual international ballet competitions called Maya
  • 2005 - Plisetskaya received Spain’s Prince of Asturias Award for the arts, and the following year she was granted the Japan Art Association’s Praemium Imperiale prize for theatre or film
  • 2015 - dies at the age of 89
